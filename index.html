Overview

The Literary Intelligence Lab (LIL) is a research and teaching initiative kindled at The Pennsylvania State University focused on the resonances between literary practice and artificial intelligence. In collaboration with students and faculty at the University of South Florida, LIL explores the boundaries and possibilities of reading, contemplation, literary experimentation and recombinant art in the era of open source texts and AI. Undergraduate research is at the core of the LIL, the classroom our laboratory for pedagogy at scale.

Literature in the LIL names a vast archive for training, enhancing, and entrancing attention. Together, we gather tactics for navigating, thriving through, and creating within ever more compressed and stressed ecologies of attention. Close and choral reading is training in immersive literacy and contemplation. Literary Intelligence here trains Artificial Intelligence, and vice versa.

The lab investigates the reading of literature as a form of pattern intelligence with long-standing methods for modeling meaning, memory, rhetoric,affect, and attention. Enchantment, terror, awe and mirth emerge from patterns of language and sound. LIL brings literary and rhetorical practice into conversation with contemporary AI systems, with particular emphasis on small language models, choral reading, and analog media.

Projects

Surfing the Wake in the W@ke of AI

Origin: Graduate Seminar, English 586

This project emerged from a Penn State graduate seminar devoted to James Joyce's Finnegans Wake. It examines the Wake as a literary system, sound archive, and source text for experimental AI research, asking what questions and practices emerge for literature in the wake of AI.

The project explores how collective human reading practices and small language models respond to a text that resists conventional interpretation.

Project components include:

  • Choral reading of Finnegans Wake, released on flexidisk
  • An analog zine featuring notes, images, and responses
  • Linear annotations and documentation
  • Finnegans Fake 2.0, a small language model trained on choral readings of the Wake and related sources
  • Radio Free Wakeitude, a ham radio transmission of Finnegans Wake in Morse Code, 24/7

Teaching

The Literary Intelligence Lab offers classes and a pedagogical environment supporting graduate and undergraduate instruction. Students interested in the Lit Lab mission are encouraged to contact us for possible collaboration. Grant writing, choral reading, hands-on work with language models, website design, basic coding, and writing email copy are just a smidgeon of the possibilities.

Zero-credit meditation workshops for training the mind for deep writing and reading will be offered each semester.

Featured courses:

  • Introduction to the Bible as Literature in the Wake of AI (English 104, JST 104) - 3 credits
  • Surfing the Wake in the W@ke of AI (English 586) - 3 credits
  • Lit Lab Intern 03 (English 496) - 3 credits
  • Sunyata for Attention Now (LIL 0) - 0 credits
