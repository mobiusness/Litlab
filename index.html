Overview

The Literary Intelligence Lab (LIL) is a research and teaching initiative kindled at The Pennsylvania State University focused on the resonances between literary study and artificial intelligence. In collaboration with students and faculty at the University of South Florida, LIL explores the boundaries and possibilities of literary experimentation and recombinant art in the era of open source texts and AI.

Literature in the LIL names a vast archive for training, enhancing, and entrancing attention. Together, we gather tactics for navigating, thriving through, and creating within ever more compressed and stressed ecologies of attention.

The lab investigates literature as a form of intelligence with long-standing methods for modeling meaning, memory, rhetoric, and attention. LIL brings literary and rhetorical analysis into conversation with contemporary AI systems, with particular emphasis on small language models and analog media practices.

Research Focus

  • Literary texts and artificial intelligence
  • Rhetorical and hermeneutic analysis of AI systems
  • Histories of authorship, privacy, and piracy
  • Literacy and attention in digital environments

Projects

Surfing the Wake in the W@ke of AI

Origin: Graduate Seminar, English 586

This project emerged from a Penn State graduate seminar devoted to James Joyce's Finnegans Wake. It examines the Wake as a literary system, sound archive, and source text for experimental AI research, asking what questions and practices emerge for literature in the wake of AI.

The project explores how collective human reading practices and small language models respond to a text that resists conventional interpretation.

Project components include:

  • Choral reading of Finnegans Wake, released on flexidisk
  • An analog zine featuring notes, images, and responses
  • Linear annotations and documentation
  • Finnegans Fake 2.0, a small language model trained on the Wake and related sources
  • Radio Free Wakeitude, a ham radio transmission of Finnegans Wake in Morse Code, 24/7

Teaching

The Literary Intelligence Lab offers classes and a pedagogical environment supporting graduate and undergraduate instruction. Students interested in the Lit Lab mission are encouraged to contact us for possible collaboration. Grant writing, hands-on work with language models, website design, basic coding, and writing email copy are just a smidgeon of the possibilities.

Zero-credit meditation workshops for training the mind for deep writing and reading will be offered each semester.

Featured courses:

  • Introduction to the Bible as Literature in the Wake of AI (English 104, JST 104) - 3 credits
  • Surfing the Wake in the W@ke of AI (English 586) - 3 credits
  • Lit Lab Intern 03 (English 496) - 3 credits
  • Sunyata for Attention Now (LIL 0) - 0 credits
